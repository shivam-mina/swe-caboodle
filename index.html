2026 note
Mid-sized companies pioneering shift away from pure LeetCode toward practical assessments
The interview bar has shifted ~1 standard deviation higher since 2021. Performance that got an offer then may not clear screening today.
What changed in 2025-2026
🧠
Systems reasoning over LeetCode speed
Interviewers increasingly hand you a failing service log or architecture and ask you to diagnose it. DSA still matters but is no longer the center of gravity. Expect retry storms, stale cache entries, cascading failures as the real problems to solve.
🤖
AI tool usage is now a standard interview question
Companies explicitly ask "How do you use Copilot/AI in your workflow?" Wrong answers: dismissing AI entirely OR blindly accepting its output. Right answer: 2 concrete stories — one where AI accelerated your work, one where you caught an AI mistake.
💻
Live coding replacing take-home tests
71% of engineering leaders say AI makes skill assessment harder. Take-homes are disappearing. Live coding is rising — you need to explain reasoning out loud, not just produce output. Interviewers are trained to probe unusually polished code.
📐
System design bar is significantly higher
Mid-level now requires both HLD and LLD. Geospatial indexing (geohashing, quadtrees), stream processing, and AI-agentic architectures appear in standard SWE rounds. RAG pipelines and LLM integration knowledge increasingly expected even for non-ML roles.
📉
Downleveling is common — prepare strategically
63% of senior candidates received downleveled offers in 2025. Always negotiate level before compensation. Bring promo docs or impact evidence from your current role. For 3-4 YOE: target mid-level, negotiate up from there.
📈
Hiring is recovering but highly selective
Big tech hiring is up ~40% year-on-year from 2023 lows. But companies are methodical — precision over speed. Breadth of skills valued over narrow specialisms. Warm introductions >> mass applying on job boards.
